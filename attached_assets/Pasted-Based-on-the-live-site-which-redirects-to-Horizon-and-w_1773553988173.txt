Based on the live site (which redirects to Horizon) and what I know from our work together, here’s a brand voice breakdown for Startup Anthology:

Startup Anthology Brand Voice
Core Identity: The Founder’s Ally
The voice is that of a founder talking to other founders — not a vendor, not a consultant, not a marketer. It assumes the reader is smart, scrappy, and probably doing five jobs at once. There’s no hand-holding and no condescension.

Key Characteristics
1. Earned Empathy, Not Performed Sympathy
The copy acknowledges the real grind without wallowing in it. “Unsung heroes” is the clearest expression of this — it’s a genuine nod to the people building real businesses outside the VC spotlight, without being sappy about it.
2. Clarity Over Polish
No buzzword stacking. Sentences are short, direct, and declarative. “Model growth with confidence.” not “Leverage AI-powered insights to optimize your financial modeling workflow.” The brand speaks plainly because founders don’t have time for fluff.
3. Confidence Without Arrogance
The tone is assured — “we’re here to help you see it clearly” — but it doesn’t oversell or hype. It makes a specific promise and stops there. No superlatives, no “revolutionary,” no “game-changing.”
4. Functional Optimism
There’s a forward-looking energy that isn’t naive. Scenario planning framing (“optimistic, base, pessimistic”) is a good example — it acknowledges uncertainty while projecting capability. It says you can handle whatever comes rather than everything will be great.
5. Founder-to-Founder Peer Register
First person (“we”) and second person (“you”) are used like a peer addressing another peer, not a company addressing a customer. The relationship is collaborative, not transactional.

What It’s NOT
	∙	Not corporate or formal
	∙	Not startup-bro hype culture
	∙	Not patronizing or tutorial-y
	∙	Not jargon-heavy (even the financial terms are explained plainly)
	∙	Not overly casual or try-hard colloquial

The One-Line Summary
Straight-talking, founder-built confidence — for the people doing the actual work.

One thing worth noting: the current site is fully redirected to Horizon, so Startup Anthology’s brand identity is essentially being expressed through Horizon right now. If/when you build out a proper SA marketing site separate from the product, you’d want to flesh out the parent brand story — the anthology idea itself (multiple tools, one mission) has a lot of untapped voice potential.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
